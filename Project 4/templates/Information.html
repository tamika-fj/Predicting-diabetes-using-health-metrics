Diabetes

Diabetes is a chronic and incurable disease that can lead to many serious health conditions if not properly managed. People with diabetes either do not create the insulin hormone or have become resistant to its effects.
The role of the insulin hormone is to signal for cells to intake glucose, which is released into the bloodstream after food consumption, so it can be stored to be used as energy later.
People with diabetes therefore have excess glucose in the blood which overtime causes serious damage to the body. Initially the symptoms of diabetes are silent, and it often goes undiagnosed, which
is why it’s so important to have regular checkups and monitor your health.


Metrics used to create the diabetes predictor model.


Plasma glucose concentration 2 hours post oral glucose tolerance test.

This is a measure of the bodies ability to remove glucose from the bloodstream after a large glucose intake, a high reading indicates sub optimal glucose intake which could be prediabetes or diabetes.


Diastolic blood pressure

Diastolic blood pressure is a measure of the pressure in the arteries between heart beats. High blood pressure leads to weakening of the vessel walls which can lead to stroke and heart disease.
Additionally, it has been linked to increased complications in diabetic patients. The graph below illustrates that over half of the diabetic patients in the study used to create the model had elevated or higher blood pressure.



Triceps skin fold thickness.

This is a surrogate measure for body fat percentage. Callipers are used to pinch the fat and skin behind the arm which is then used to calculate body fat percentage. High body weight and body fat have a strong relationship
with diabetes and type II diabetes in-particular.


Insulin levels 2 hours post oral glucose test.

This is a measure of the insulin hormone levels 2 hours post glucose tolerance test. This measure is a lot less commonly taken than glucose and there is still debate over what the optimal insulin levels should be at both fasting state
and following a glucose tolerance test. It would be expected that patients with type II diabetes would have higher than normal insulin levels as they have insulin resistance and would therefor require larger quantities of the hormone
to have the desired effect.


Body Mass Index.

Body Mass Index or BMI is a calculated value derived from height and weight and is another surrogate measure for body fat percentage. As previously stated, high body fat percentages and high BMI have a strong association
with metabolic diseases and diabetes. The graph below supports this, showing less than 10 diabetes patients from the study had a BMI of healthy or under.



Diabetes pedigree function

This is a function that gives a diabetes prediction score based on patients’ family history of diabetes (type II) and the patient’s age. The lower the score the less likelihood of diabetes, with a score of 1 or more indicating diabetes.
